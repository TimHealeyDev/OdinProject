Thundercat

Stephen Bruner

Master bassist.

Here's a timeline of Thundercat's life so far:

  • 1984 - Born Los Angeles, California.
  • 1999 - By age 15 he already had a minor hit in Germany as a member of the boy band No Curfew.
  • 2000 - Joined his brother as a member of Los Angeles punk band Suicidal Tendencies.
  • 2005 - Appeared on Kamasi Washington's Live at 5th Street Dick's, one of his earliest album appearances.
  • 2007 - Appeared again on Kamasi Washington's album The Proclamation.
  • 2008 - Collaborated on Erykah Badu's New Amerykah.
  • 2010 - Collaborated with Flying Lotus on their album Cosmogramma.
  • 2011 - Released his first solo album, The Golden Age of Apocalypse.
  • 2012 - Became a consistent collaborator with Flying Lotus with contributions on their albums; Until the Quiet Comes (2012), and You're Dead! (2014).
  • 2013 - Released his second album Apocalypse.
  • 2015 - Played a major role in the creation of Kendrick Lamar's critically acclaimed album To Pimp a Butterfly.
  • 2016 - Performed on stage with Red Hot Chilli Peppers, playing additional bass on their song Go Robot.
  • 2017 - Released his third album Drunk, appearing on The Tonight Show Starring Jimmy Fallon to promote it.
  • 2018 - Played a major role in his long-time friend and frequent collaborator, Mac Miller's Grammy nominated album Swimming.
  • 2020 - Released his fourth album It Is What It Is, dedicating the album to his friend Mac Miller.

“It’s like seeing a great painter with a canvas that looks like a lot of nothing, and then one little stroke goes and you’re like, ‘Wow you saw that the whole time?’ He hears things in songs that other people don’t hear.”

~ Kamasi Washington
